10. Kochikame

10.Kochikame

Kochikame ,is a Japanese comedy manga series written and illustrated by Osamu Akimoto and revolves around the misadventures of middle-aged cop Kankichi Ryotsu.

Since its inception on September 1976 it has over 1800 chapters collected into 199 tankobon volumes, making it the manga series with the highest number of volumes. The manga has been adapted into a television anime series two theatrical animated films, two live-action movies, several stage adaptations, and a live-action television series.

TV Asahi named the anime number 36 on its list of the Top 100 Anime.

8. Doraemon

8.Doraemon

Doraemon TV series is based on the manga by team Fujiko Fujio.First published in December 1969 this anime has been recognised as one of the most popular anime of all time.

Doraemon is about a futuristic robot cat who is there to help a pre-teen kid named Nobita.This anime is packed with futuristic gadgets,funny storyline and cool songs

7.Ninja Hattori

7.Ninja Hattori

Ninja Hattori-kun, known as Ninja Hattori in some countries, is a manga series created by Motoo Abiko, later adapted into a television drama, an anime series

Kenichi Mitsuba is an average kid who goes to secondary school and struggles with his studies, he is very stubborn and is very lazy but he takes the help of his ninja friend Hattori Kanzo to get out of any difficult situation.

This anime was remade as a 2013 anime series as a joint venture between India and Japan and is currently on the air in several Asian countries.


6.Digimon

6.Digimon

This franchise was first created in 1997 as a series of virtual pets(Digimon),which are monsters living in a "Digital World, a parallel universe that originated from Earth's various communication networks.

A group of young teens is unexpectedly sent to the mysterious Digital World and paired up with their own powerful, morphing monster called the Digimon. Together the entire group set out on an adventure to fight evil and save the world.

Almost all villains in Digimon Adventure come back at least one more time after the presumed death.


5.Yu-Gi-Oh

5.Yu-Gi-Oh

Yu-Gi-Oh! (1998) is the first anime adaptation of the popular manga by Kazuki Takahashi.

Yugi Mutou is a shy young boy who has finally completed his grandfather's Thousand-Year puzzle. Little does he realize that this puzzle unleashes an alter ego.Yu-Gi-Oh is on his way to to become the best dual master of monster cards and his alter ego and his friends help him on his way.

In Japanese, Yu-Gi-Oh means King of Games.


4.Naruto

4.Naruto

The series is based on a one-shot manga by Kishimoto that was published in the August 1997 issue of Akamaru Jump.

Follows the struggles of three young ninjas - Naruto Uzumaki, Sasuke Uchiha and Sakura Haruno - as they go through their training.Naruto has a sealed demon fox(nine tails) inside him, he grew up with no family, and the villagers hated him thinking that he himself was the demon fox. Naruto's dream is to become Hokage, and have the villagers acknowledge him.

The voice actors doing the voice of Naruto both in English and Japanese are both female.


3.Dragonball-Z

3.Dragonball-Z

Dragon Ball manga series was created by Akira Toriyama, that were published from 1988 to 1995 in Weekly Shonen Jump.

After learning that he is from another planet, a warrior named Goku and his friends are prompted to defend it from an onslaught of extraterrestrial enemies.

Freeza and Goku's fight in Namek is the longest battle in anime history, with a duration of a little over four hours

2.Beyblade

In these battles, everyone has his/her special moves and have some special type of Beyblade like Tyson which has Dragoon type beyblade beast. The Bladebreakers' bit-beasts are based on the 4 Chinese Gods which are: Dragoon - Seriyu the Dragon, Dranzer - Suzaku the Phoenix, Draciel - Genbu the Turtle and Driger - Byako the Tiger


1.Pokemon

1.Pokemon

The adventures of Ash Ketchum and his partner Pikachu, who travel across many regions in hopes of being regarded as a Pokemon master.

Ten-year-old Ash aspires to be the greatest Pokémon (pocket monster) trainer in the world. To do this, he enlists the help of two friends, Misty and Brock, and his own Pokémon, Pikachu. Together, they must search their world for all 150 Pokémon, while avoiding the likes of Team Rocket and their Pokémon, Meowth.

The names of two Pokemon, Ekans and Arbok, are reversed words.
